## Project Overview

**TYRANNO** is a speculative communication system designed for a 2050 scenario in which humans and *Tyrannosaurus rex* must coexist in urban environments. The project asks how communication might be built when spoken language is not available and when the two species perceive movement, posture, sound, and space differently.

The project combines speculative design, semiotics, typography, behavioral research, and interaction design. Rather than treating type as a purely phonetic system, TYRANNO develops a visual language grounded in gesture, posture, motion, and behavioral cues.

## Core Concept

The central idea is that communication can be constructed from observable behavior rather than speech. TYRANNO translates recurring actions and signals into a symbolic system that can function as a shared visual vocabulary between humans and a non-human species.

The concept draws from:

- semiotic models of icon, index, and symbol
- hieroglyphic and logographic writing systems
- non-verbal communication and gesture
- speculative design methods
- research into T. rex anatomy, movement, posture, tail position, head direction, gait, and vocalization analogies

## Editorial Narrative Mapping

### Question

How could humans communicate with a species that does not share human language?

### Context

The project is situated in a speculative 2050 urban scenario shaped by advances in genetic engineering and de-extinction. The return of an extinct predator creates practical and ethical questions about coexistence, interpretation, safety, and communication.

### Research

The research combines semiotics, typography, speculative design, paleontology, non-verbal communication, and practice-based design methods. It examines how signs create meaning and how movement, anatomy, posture, and spatial behavior might become a basis for shared understanding.

### Concept Development

The symbolic language was developed by identifying behavioral cues and translating them into visual units. The system does not imitate conventional spoken language. Instead, it builds meaning from body orientation, movement, repetition, direction, rhythm, and relational context.

### Process

The process moved through research, behavioral mapping, visual abstraction, glyph prototyping, classification, system expansion, and digital testing. The project was refined through iterative comparison between biological reference, semiotic function, legibility, and interaction potential.

### Communication System

TYRANNO operates as a non-phonetic communication system. Its signs may work as icons, indexes, or learned symbols depending on context. The system is intended to support recognition, warning, instruction, orientation, and basic interspecies exchange.

### Outcome

The outcome is a speculative yet functional communication framework presented through typography, editorial design, motion, and interaction. The website extends the system beyond static display by demonstrating symbols, translating behavior, and introducing the language through learning scenarios.

### Reflection

The project reframes typography as a communication system that can exist beyond human speech. It shows how speculative design can connect research, narrative, visual form, and interaction while also exposing the limits of designing for a species that cannot participate directly in the design process.
