# Purpose

This document defines the foundation of the Portfolio Operating System.

It serves as the highest-level specification for the project and establishes the principles that guide every future decision, including content, design, interaction, implementation, and long-term development.

All subsequent documents should be considered extensions of this foundation.

---

# Project Definition

This project is not a conventional portfolio website.

It is the first public interface of an evolving communication archive that documents a long-term creative practice.

Rather than presenting finished work alone, the system reveals how ideas are researched, developed, communicated, and transformed into visual outcomes.

The portfolio is therefore treated as an operating system instead of a collection of projects.

---

# Identity

Kylie Bi is a Communication Designer and Creative Producer working across visual communication, concept development, creative direction, and interdisciplinary storytelling.

The practice focuses on transforming abstract ideas into structured communication systems through research, narrative, visual language, and production.

Projects may vary in medium, but they share one common objective:

Design communication that makes complex ideas understandable, engaging, and meaningful.

---

# Mission

Build a communication archive that demonstrates not only what has been created, but also how creative thinking is structured.

The archive should encourage curiosity before explanation and communication before presentation.

---

# Long-term Vision

The Portfolio Operating System is intended to become a continuously evolving knowledge system rather than a static portfolio.

Over time it may include:

• professional projects

• speculative research

• exhibitions

• publications

• experiments

• writing

• visual investigations

• talks

• collaborative work

The website should be capable of expanding without changing its underlying structure.

---

# Current Objective

The immediate objective is to deliver a professional portfolio that supports applications within the European creative industry.

Every design decision should therefore balance two requirements:

1. Deliver a high-quality portfolio efficiently.

2. Establish a scalable foundation for future expansion.

Short-term execution should never compromise long-term flexibility.

---

# Core Principles

## Communication before Decoration

Visual decisions must support communication.

Aesthetic choices should strengthen meaning rather than exist independently.

---

## Systems before Objects

Projects are presented as parts of larger thinking systems rather than isolated visual artifacts.

The archive communicates relationships instead of collections.

---

## Process before Outcome

Whenever appropriate, development, research, experimentation, and iteration are treated as equally important as final results.

---

## Curiosity before Explanation

The interface should encourage visitors to ask questions before providing answers.

Information is progressively revealed instead of immediately displayed.

---

## Consistency before Novelty

The archive should maintain a coherent visual language across projects.

Individual projects may differ in content, but the overall system should remain recognizable.

---

## Longevity before Trend

Design decisions should prioritize clarity, flexibility, and long-term relevance over temporary visual trends.

---

# Success Criteria

The project succeeds when visitors understand the designer through the communication system rather than through self-description.

After exploring the archive, visitors should leave with a clear understanding of:

• how ideas are developed

• how research becomes communication

• how communication becomes visual systems

rather than simply remembering individual projects.

# Scope

Included

• Website strategy

• Design system

• Project documentation

• Visual language

• Portfolio content

• Development specifications

• Decision history

Excluded

• Temporary notes

• Brainstorming

• Incomplete experiments

• Personal reminders

These belong to the working process rather than the Portfolio Operating System itself.
