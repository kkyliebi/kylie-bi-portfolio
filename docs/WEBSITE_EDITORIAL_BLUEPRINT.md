# Website Editorial Blueprint

**Status:** 🌳 Core structure / 🌿 copy in development

## Purpose

Define what each part of the portfolio must communicate before final visual design and production copy are added.

The website is a horizontal editorial journey on desktop and a clear vertical reading experience on smaller screens.

## Core journey

**Landing → About → Questions / Archive → Five selected projects → Contact**

This is not a conventional menu-led portfolio where projects appear as disconnected thumbnails. The reader should move through a designed sequence and understand the projects as evidence of one practice.

## 1. Landing

### Responsibility

Establish the portfolio as a communication archive and introduce a way of thinking before introducing biography.

### Must communicate

- Kylie Bi
- Communication Designer & Creative Producer
- Visual Communication + Creative Direction + Concept Development
- the recurring method: discover, connect, translate, materialise
- a reason to continue exploring

### Reader question

**What kind of designer is this, and why is the portfolio structured differently?**

### Avoid

- long biography
- generic claims such as “passionate multidisciplinary designer”
- a crowded project grid
- decorative motion with no editorial role

## 2. About

### Responsibility

Translate the initial impression into a credible professional identity.

### Must communicate

- how Kylie works across concept, narrative, visual communication and production
- the connection between academic/speculative practice and professional brand communication experience
- her ability to move from abstract ideas to executable systems
- current location and professional availability when approved

### Reader question

**What can she contribute to a team, studio or commission?**

## 3. Questions / Archive

### Responsibility

Introduce the selected projects through the questions they investigate, rather than through software, format or category alone.

### Format principle

The preferred label is **Questions** or a related editorial title rather than a conventional “Table of Contents.”

Each project entry should contain:

- one lead question
- one concise project synopsis
- category / context
- a direct route into the case

The question creates curiosity. The synopsis gives enough orientation without resolving the project before it is opened.

### Reader question

**What kinds of problems does Kylie choose to investigate?**

## 4. Project sequence

The first employable version should prioritise five projects that collectively prove range without weakening the central positioning.

Current working set:

1. **Tyranno Typeface** — speculative visual language and design research
2. **ScentSync** — speculative service, product and communication system
3. **Madam, in Eden, I’m Adam** — visual narrative and photography
4. **APRICA AROUND** — identity, interface and campaign proposal
5. **Audi selected cases** — brand communication and creative production

The final fifth-project packaging and exact titles remain subject to content audit.

## 5. Shared project-page structure

Every case should follow the same underlying reading logic while allowing different visual expression.

### A. Lead-in question

Open with the central question or tension.

### B. Context

Explain the situation, brief or speculative premise.

### C. Insight / Research

Show only the research that directly changed the work.

### D. Concept development

Reveal the central idea and the reasoning behind it.

### E. System / Process

Show how the concept became a communication system, experience or production plan.

### F. Outcome

Present the strongest final artefacts in a deliberate sequence.

### G. Role and contribution

Make personal responsibility unmistakable, especially for collaborative and professional projects.

### H. Reflection

State what the project proves, what changed through the process, or where the system could develop next.

## 6. Contact

### Responsibility

Convert interest into a clear next action.

### Must include

- concise closing statement
- email
- selected professional links
- CV access when ready

### Reader question

**How do I contact or hire her?**

## Reading modes

The website must support two professional behaviours:

### Curated reading

A reader follows the full horizontal sequence and experiences the intended narrative.

### Fast scanning

A reader can identify positioning, selected projects, role, outcomes and contact details quickly.

Neither mode should break the other.

## Minimum viable editorial version

The first job-ready release is complete when:

- landing positioning is final enough to publish
- About copy is credible and concise
- five project entries have approved titles, lead questions, roles and summaries
- at least three strongest projects have complete case narratives and usable media
- remaining selected projects have concise but honest case pages
- contact details and CV route are present
- no section contains internal planning language or unexplained placeholders

## Editorial rule

Do not add a section because portfolios usually contain it. Every section must answer a reader question or advance the professional argument.
