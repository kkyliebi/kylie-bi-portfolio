> Canonical status: Portfolio Knowledge Base v1.0. This version consolidates confirmed professional experience into one integrated portfolio case. All campaign-specific claims, dates, metrics, and ownership boundaries must be checked against original records before publication.

## Project Overview

**Audi Integrated Brand Communication** brings together selected professional work developed across brand communication, content, events, and creative production. The project is structured as one portfolio case with several possible sub-cases, including **Audi Channel**, **Audi A8L Horch Global Premiere**, and **Audi × Ryuichi Sakamoto**.

The portfolio purpose is not to present every task completed for Audi. It is to show how brand strategy, creative development, communication planning, production coordination, and execution were connected across different formats and audiences.

## Context / Question

How can a global automotive brand maintain a coherent identity while communicating through launches, cultural collaborations, editorial content, live experiences, and digital channels?

Each sub-case should later define its precise brief, audience, market, and communication challenge from verified source documents.

## Concept Development

The work required translating brand priorities into communication ideas that could operate across content and experience. The conceptual process included identifying the central message, adapting it to the communication context, and aligning tone, visual direction, storytelling, and production requirements with the Audi brand.

Across the case, the narrative should emphasize:

- integration rather than isolated deliverables
- consistency across touchpoints
- the relationship between brand strategy and creative execution
- collaboration among client, creative, production, media, and event teams
- adaptation of global brand language to specific communication moments

## Research / Process

Confirmed areas of contribution include brand communication, PR and event planning, creative production, and coordination between stakeholders and execution teams.

The process may be mapped as:

1. understand the communication objective and brand context
2. define or refine the creative and narrative direction
3. translate the direction into content, event, campaign, or channel requirements
4. coordinate across client, creative, production, and implementation teams
5. review outputs for brand consistency and practical delivery
6. support launch, publication, presentation, or live execution

Precise responsibilities must be assigned separately for each sub-case after source verification.
