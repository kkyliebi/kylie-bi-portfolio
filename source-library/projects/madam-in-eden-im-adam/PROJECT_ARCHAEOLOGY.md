# Madam, in Eden, I'm Adam — Project Archaeology

> Review status: first editorial excavation pass based on the current canonical record. This document identifies what must be clarified before the case can move from a polished photographic series to a distinctive narrative project.

## Current Strength

Madam already shows direct involvement across concept, storyboard, shooting, performance, sequencing, and post-production. It is the clearest image-led case in the portfolio and demonstrates control of atmosphere and visual continuity.

Its deeper value may lie in how a simple recurring bodily gesture becomes a narrative device across multiple frames.

## What Is Still Too Generic

- The meaning of the title is not explained.
- The central narrative, conflict, or emotional transformation remains unclear.
- “Identity,” “body language,” “cyclical narrative,” and “symbolic visual relationships” appear in supporting records, but the current canonical account does not verify how they operate in the images.
- The role of the hand is described as recurring, but not interpreted.
- The reason for the chosen sequence, props, setting, and tonal treatment is not yet documented.
- Collaborator roles and the exact extent of direction versus execution remain to be confirmed.

## Hidden Soul to Test

Possible deeper proposition:

> The series uses the hand as both subject and narrator, turning repeated gesture into a cyclical visual language about identity, intimacy, control, or transformation.

This is only a hypothesis until the image sequence and original concept are reviewed.

## Questions for Kylie

1. Why is the project called “Madam, in Eden, I’m Adam”? Is the palindrome structurally important?
2. What is the story or emotional arc of the series?
3. What does the hand represent within the project?
4. Why did you choose to perform as the hand model yourself?
5. Are there two identities, mirrored roles, gendered positions, or a cyclical relationship in the narrative?
6. What does “Eden” mean in this context?
7. Which visual symbols, props, colours, or gestures carry specific meaning?
8. Is the sequence reversible or circular, like the title?
9. What was planned in the storyboard and what emerged during the shoot?
10. Who photographed the images, and who directed framing, lighting, props, and performance?
11. How did you select the final images?
12. What did the saturation, contrast, and tonal adjustments need to communicate?
13. Was the final work intended as a photo essay, fashion/editorial story, gallery sequence, book, or presentation?
14. Which image is the narrative turning point, and why?
15. What should a viewer feel or question after seeing the final frame?
