# Project Identity

**Status:** 🌳 Core

## Working title

**Kylie Bi — Creative Communication Cases Archive**

## What this portfolio is

This portfolio is not a neutral container for finished work. It is an editorial communication system that shows how Kylie Bi turns abstract questions into structured, visual and experiential outcomes.

The projects are evidence of one recurring practice:

> discover → connect → translate → materialise

## Professional positioning

Kylie Bi is positioned as a **Communication Designer & Creative Producer** working across:

- visual communication
- creative direction
- concept development
- narrative systems
- editorial and exhibition thinking
- brand and cultural communication

The portfolio should make clear that she does not only produce visuals. She builds communication systems that connect research, narrative, people, media and execution.

## Primary audience

- Creative Directors
- Studio Founders
- Communication Designers
- Creative Producers
- Exhibition and Editorial Designers
- Brand Strategists
- Cultural institutions and interdisciplinary studios

Priority markets include Milan, Amsterdam, Berlin and Copenhagen.

## Immediate purpose

The current priority is to create the minimum complete, credible portfolio required to begin job applications quickly.

The system must remain expandable, but long-term completeness must not delay the first employable version.

## Desired reader impression

After 20–30 seconds, the reader should understand:

> Kylie does not simply make visuals. She develops concepts and turns them into coherent communication systems.

## Editorial character

The portfolio should feel:

- curated rather than accumulated
- precise rather than over-explained
- intelligent without becoming academic
- calm, contemporary and visually distinctive
- open enough to create curiosity
- structured enough to support fast professional review

## Non-goals

This portfolio should not present Kylie primarily as:

- a junior graphic designer
- a software specialist
- a decorative image-maker
- a collection of unrelated student assignments
- a long-form thesis archive with no professional framing

## Governing decision

When speed and completeness conflict, prioritise the version that is strong enough for applications and can be improved without restructuring the whole system.
