## 1. Project Overview

**Deear Magazine** is an editorial design project focused on translating content into a coherent visual and reading system. It demonstrates how typography, hierarchy, image sequencing, pacing, and page architecture can shape the experience of a publication.

Within the portfolio, the project should be presented not simply as a set of attractive spreads, but as evidence of editorial thinking: how information was organized, how a consistent visual language was established, and how individual pages contribute to the rhythm of the full publication.

## 2. Context / Question

## 4. Research / Process

The editorial process can be documented through the following stages once the original files are available:

1. review and classify the available content
2. identify content hierarchy and reading order
3. define the grid, typography, margins, and recurring page elements
4. test image scale, crop, sequencing, and text-image relationships
5. develop page families for different article or content types
6. refine pacing across opening pages, features, transitions, and closing material
7. prepare the final publication and presentation outputs
